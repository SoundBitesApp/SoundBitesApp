SoundBites

Orchestrate Your Plate.
An Experiment with Music, Food, & Synesthesia

What Is SoundBites?

SoundBites is an app that matches up food with music

For example, Lemon Meringue Pie almost always starts in A major - wouldn't it be nice if the perfect piece of music could augment your enjoyment? Or perhaps that cucumber salad would taste even better with a bit of Miles Davis in the background.

SoundBites helps you find the music to match your tastes, and vice-versa. This makes thinking about music and food fun and interesting - and might make fussy eaters try new foods if only to find out what they sound like.

It also helps people understand taste synesthesia.

How It Works

1. Plate It.

Tell SoundBites what you're eating, or take take a picture of your food.

2. Rate It.

Now Rate your food using various categories. How sweet is it? Is it Salty? Spicy? Crunchy? Does it taste bitter?

3. Hear It.

SoundBites searches its database and finds music to match your food.

4. Train It.

Train SoundBites with your musical preferences to get better recommendations. You can also suggest music/food pairings of your own.

As people train SoundBites with their musical tastes, it begins to see patterns in how people think about food and music. The idea would be to collect a massive database - perhaps one day we might be able to make recipes based on particular tunes, but in the meantime it'll be interesting to see what happens...

The whole idea is to make thinking about music and food fun and interesting.

Status

SoundBites is just getting started!

History

It began with donuts.

During the wee hours of the Boston Music Techfest 2014 hackathon we music hackers paused to have breakfast. While we were munching LJ mentioned that she heard different kinds of music whenever she ate.

Musical tastes? Interesting.

We started feeding LJ a steady stream of donuts, and she played what she heard on the piano. Shimmery arpeggios for chocolate glaze, sedated minor keys for doughy plain donuts.

This was fascinating! We thought about how interesting it would be to find out what other people heard when they taste.

Soon LJ, Danny, and Alex worked up some mockups, and thus was born the idea for SoundBites.
